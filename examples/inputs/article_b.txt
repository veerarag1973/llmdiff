Large language models (LLMs) are a category of foundation models trained on
immense amounts of text data to understand and generate human-like text.
They learned to predict the next word in sequences of text from vast corpora
scraped from the internet, books, and other sources.  By scaling up the
number of parameters and training data, researchers discovered that these
models exhibited emergent capabilities — such as reasoning, translation, and
code generation — that were not explicitly programmed.
